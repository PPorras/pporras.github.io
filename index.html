About

I am a mathematician working at the intersection of dynamical systems, Hamiltonian mechanics, and KAM theory. My research focuses on the development of constructive methods and high-performance algorithms to analyze invariant structures, such as Lagrangian tori, in time-dependent quasi-periodic Hamiltonian systems.

One of my main contributions is the advancement of computational and mathematically rigorous techniques in KAM theory, including:

  • Developing parameterization methods to compute Lagrangian invariant tori
  • Computer-assisted proofs (CAPs) to provide mathematically rigorous error bounds in KAM Theory
  • Studying persistence and breakdown of tori under perturbations
  • Applying KAM theory to magnetic confinement in Tokamaks to better understand plasma stability

My research bridges the gap between mathematical theory and computational applications, contributing to the rigorous analysis of dynamical systems. By combining advanced mathematical techniques with high-performance computation, I aim to develop new methodologies with direct impact in applied physics and engineering.
